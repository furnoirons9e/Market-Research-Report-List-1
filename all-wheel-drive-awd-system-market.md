<p><p>All-wheel drive (AWD) systems are categorized into two main types: manual and automatic. Manual AWD allows the driver to engage or disengage the system as needed, providing flexibility and control in challenging conditions. In contrast, automatic AWD systems continuously monitor road conditions and seamlessly distribute power to all wheels without driver intervention, enhancing convenience and performance. Both types improve vehicle traction and stability, catering to diverse driving preferences and environments, making them popular choices in various vehicle markets.</p></p>

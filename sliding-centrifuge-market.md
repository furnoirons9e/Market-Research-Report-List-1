<p><p>The sliding centrifuge market is segmented into low-speed and high-speed centrifuges, each serving distinct applications. Low-speed centrifuges operate at lower RPMs, ideal for separating larger particles in processes like blood processing and sedimentation. In contrast, high-speed centrifuges operate at elevated RPMs, facilitating faster separation of smaller particles in applications such as cell culture and biochemical assays. Both types are essential in laboratories and industrial settings, catering to varying needs in sample processing and separation efficiency.</p></p>
